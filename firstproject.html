WORK FROM HOME ERGONOMICS SELF-ASSESSMENT

Answer the following questions with the best of your ability.

1. My chair is comfortable and working appropriately.

2. When I lean against the backrest, my feet are fully supported by the floor.

3. My chair provides support for my lower back.

4. When using the backrest, I can sit without the chair edge pressing into my thighs or the backside of my knees.

5. My elbows stay close to my sides and my wrists are mostly neutral (not bent) when I use my keyboard and mouse.

Many keyboards have foldable feet under them. Sometimes it helps to flatten the feet or sometimes, to leave the keyboard at an angle. Pay attention to what helps make your wrists straighter.

6. My mouse is the same level and next to my keyboard.

7. I adjusted the screen brightness, contrast and font size so my eyes are comfortable when looking at the screen.

8. My mousing hand and arm feels good, without aches or pains.

9. My monitor is located directly in front of me. My neck is in neutral and not rotated.

10. I can lean against my backrest and see the screen clearly without leaning forward.

The correct distance from the user is dependent on the size of the monitor, the font, screen resolution and the individual user (e.g. vision and use of bifocals or progressive eyeglasses).

11. I don’t wear glasses and the top of my monitor is near eye level.

Or— I wear progressive lenses or bifocals and the screen is low enough for me to view it without tilting my chin up

12. I have sufficient legroom under my desk. Nothing under the desk encroaches into my leg space, or compromises my posture or could cause a tripping hazard.

13. Items that I use frequently are located close to me. Items that I use less often are in the “occasional work” area.

14. I take mini-pauses to relieve static posture and to rest my eyes. I alternate between sitting and standing, or move around before I get tired or have discomfort.

15. I don’t have any trip hazards around my work area, such as wieldy cables, mats, piles of things on the floor, etc.

16. If I use the phone while writing or keying, I use a headset or speakerphone.

17. I can look at reference documents while typing without bending my neck very much.

18. I use a laptop computer for long periods of time

19. I don't have neck problems when looking at the monitor for a long period.

20 I don't feel discomfort due to static body posture for a long time.
